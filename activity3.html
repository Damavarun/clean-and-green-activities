Biomass-Based Biogas Generation System

Block Diagram

Working Principle & Technical Details

Source of Energy: Biomass from cattle manure, agricultural residues, food waste, and other organic materials that contain complex organic compounds suitable for anaerobic digestion processes in controlled environments.

Energy Conversion Process: Organic feedstock undergoes anaerobic digestion in sealed digesters where microorganisms break down biodegradable material in oxygen-free conditions. This biological process produces biogas (60-70% methane, 30-40% CO2) and digestate. Gas purification systems remove impurities before the biogas is stored or utilized for various energy applications.

System Output: Produces methane-rich biogas for cooking, heating, electricity generation, and vehicle fuel. The nutrient-rich digestate byproduct serves as high-quality organic fertilizer, completing the nutrient cycle and enhancing soil health without chemical inputs.

Real-World Application: Converts organic waste into renewable energy while solving waste management challenges. Supports circular economy principles by transforming waste streams into valuable resources, reducing greenhouse gas emissions from organic decomposition, and providing decentralized energy solutions for rural communities and agricultural operations worldwide.
